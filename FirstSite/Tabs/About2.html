MicroRealities Gaming


The main reason for MicroRealities existence is nothing less than to change the way gaming software is created, written, and supported.
This may be what motivates all game designers.

The VaPORS project is also strongly motivated by the desire to,
enable and help people exercise their creativity in ways constructive and cooperative.
This motivation offers not only a diverse and rich "in game" experience, but hopefully will create a responsive and inspired community of VaPORS professionals, enthusiasts, and contributors. Following closely behind this motivation is the idea and hope that by contributing work, artists (graphic, music, etc.), developers (applications, scenarios, etc.), and servicers (sponsors, helpers, etc.) can create a broad community. And that this community can publish and financially support themselves by way of "out of game" VaPORS products and services.

Previous to this time (March 2, 1999), no outside investing was considered. This decision to wait, made a few years ago, was to ensure that the concept of VaPORS would not be lost or diluted along it's way to implementation. Now that the main concepts of a true virtual reality role-played game have been proven to work well, funding will soon be addressed; once the secondary "proofs" are implemented and nailed down over the next few months.

Because the VaPORS design is not tied to any particular genre and offers expansions quite far reaching, it never the less will first exist in the "fantasy" genre. But it will not follow the familiar "Dungeons and Dragons", or other board game, characterizations. Instead it will be based on character perception and pseudo-physics to set the stage for discovery and predictable interactions with environments of "fantasy", "horror", "sci-fi", etc. Note that if a designer chooses to, they can restrict and turn off many world features in order to make a classic "Dungeons and Dragons" style game.
